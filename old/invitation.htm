“Send A Greeting To Our Troops
In Iraq.”
By Sgt. Ted Mickelson
“Voices” Director of Circulation.
© ’06 by HAVSHI, Inc.

I remember when I was in the military during the Vietnam War. The most anticipated part of the day in my unit was “Mail Call.” Everything stopped, except the war, until the mail was handed out. Nothing, not even “incoming” could stop the mail. A letter from home was worth far more to us than all the riches in the world. It was the best reminder of all as to why we were here.

Every letter was eagerly read and then re-read a thousand more times. A package from home was like a gift sent from heaven. Any photo, the new baby, the kids, mom, dad, is worth more to a soldier than anything else you could imagine.

Any memento that reminds him of home is bound to be his most treasured possession. He’ll keep it in his helmet, hang it around his neck with his dog tags, or stuff it in his flak jacket, close to his heart. He’ll guard it with his life and protect it like he would his buddies.

Mail from home whether from family, friends, wives, girlfriends, the children, strangers, anybody, is still the best morale builder a soldier could receive and even more so during the holidays.

Nothing boosts the morale of soldiers at war like mail from home.

By joining our letter writing campaign to our troops around the world, you are letting them know you support them 100%. It says better than any other way, that you care. Your personal greeting says that you cared enough to write. There is no cost but the stamp. Download our envelope and our greeting card, write in your greeting (use the back if you need more space), fold, staple, stamp and mail. The designated sponsored unit will receive your greeting via APO in about 7 days. By participating in “ Operation Greetings From Home ”, you are also extending an invitation for a soldier to correspond with you in return. Keeping in touch with someone at home is just as important to a soldier at war as hearing from someone at home. Knowing you have done your part to comfort out troops is the best reward of all. Because the objective of “ Operation Greetings From Home ” is to generate as much mail for our troops as possible, tell your family, friends, neighbors and co-workers about it, and ask them to participate. Everyone can help bring some cheer to a soldier far from home. (Make your greeting “extra special” and have the entire family sign it, and don’t forget the dog.) Are you a teacher? Adopt-A-Soldier and make it a class project. Work in an office? Write your greeting and have all of your co-workers sign it. Belong to a club or fraternal organization? Pass it around at the next group meeting for all of the members to sign.

Remember, a greeting from home is the best morale booster a soldier can get.
Thanx, Ted.
